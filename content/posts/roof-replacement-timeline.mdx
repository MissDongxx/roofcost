# Roof Replacement Timeline: What to Expect Step by Step

Understanding the roof replacement timeline helps you plan accordingly and reduces stress during the project. Here's a complete breakdown of what to expect from start to finish.

## Overview: Total Project Timeline

| Project Phase | Duration | Notes |
|---------------|----------|-------|
| Initial Inspection | 1-2 hours | Professional assessment |
| Getting Estimates | 1-2 weeks | Schedule 2-3 contractors |
| Selection & Contract | 3-7 days | Review and sign agreement |
| Material Ordering | 1-4 weeks | Special orders may take longer |
| Permit Acquisition | 1-2 weeks | Contractor typically handles |
| Scheduling | 1-2 weeks | Weather and availability dependent |
| Installation | 1-3 days | Main construction phase |
| Final Inspection | 1-2 hours | Quality verification |

**Total Timeline**: 4-10 weeks from first call to completion

## Phase 1: Initial Inspection (Day 1)

### What Happens:
- Roofing contractor arrives and assesses your roof
- Takes measurements (often using drone technology)
- Documents damage with photos
- Discusses material options
- Identifies potential issues (deck damage, ventilation needs)

### What You Should Do:
- Be present to ask questions
- Share any concerns about problem areas
- Mention future plans (solar panels, additions)
- Show current or past leak locations

### Duration: 1-2 hours

## Phase 2: Getting Estimates (Weeks 1-2)

### Recommended Process:
1. **Contact 3-4 licensed contractors**
2. **Schedule inspections within a week**
3. **Receive written estimates** (3-7 days after inspection)
4. **Compare proposals carefully**

### What to Compare:
- Material specifications (brand, type, warranty)
- Labor and workmanship warranty
- Timeline and start date
- Total cost breakdown
- Payment schedule
- Insurance and license verification

### Duration: 1-2 weeks

## Phase 3: Selection & Contract (Weeks 2-3)

### Steps:
1. **Select your contractor** based on reputation, not just price
2. **Review contract carefully** ensuring:
   - Material specifications are detailed
   - Start and completion dates are listed
   - Payment schedule is clear
   - Warranty information is included
   - Permit responsibility is defined
   - Clean-up procedures are specified

3. **Sign contract and pay deposit** (typically 10-30%)

### Duration: 3-7 days

## Phase 4: Material Ordering & Permits (Weeks 3-7)

### Contractor Tasks:
- **Order materials** based on your selections
- **Obtain building permits** from local municipality
- **Schedule delivery** and installation crew

### Your Tasks:
- **Approve material samples** if requested
- **Confirm color selections**
- **Prepare property** (move vehicles, clear driveway)
- **Notify neighbors** about upcoming work

### Duration: 1-4 weeks (varies by material availability)

## Phase 5: Installation Day (Days 1-3)

### Day 1: Tear-Off and Preparation

**Morning (7-9 AM):**
- Crew arrives and protects property (tarping, plywood)
- Began tear-off of old roofing
- Delivered materials arrive

**Mid-Day:**
- Complete tear-off
- Inspect and repair roof deck
- Install ice and water barrier in valleys and around penetrations

**Afternoon:**
- Install underlayment across entire roof
- Begin installing flashing around chimneys, vents, and walls

### Day 2: Installation

**All Day:**
- Install starter shingles
- Install field shingles (main roofing material)
- Install ridge cap shingles
- Install ventilation components
- Complete all flashing work

### Day 3 (if needed):

**Morning:**
- Complete any remaining installation work
- Install specialty items (skylights, chimney caps)

**Afternoon:**
- Final clean-up
- Magnetic sweep for nails
- Remove debris and dumpster
- Final walkthrough with homeowner

### What You Should Expect:
- **Noise**: Very loud during tear-off (use headphones, plan to be out)
- **Vibration**: May feel inside the home
- **Workers**: 4-8 crew members typically
- **Debris**: Constant during tear-off, cleaned up throughout

### What You Should Do:
- **Keep pets indoors** for their safety
- **Remove vehicles** from driveway
- **Keep children away** from work area
- **Be available** for questions
- **Take photos** before, during, and after

## Phase 6: Final Inspection & Walkthrough (Day 3-4)

### Final Inspection:
- Contractor reviews completed work
- Checks all flashing and penetration seals
- Verifies proper ventilation
- Ensures clean-up is complete

### Homeowner Walkthrough:
- Review all work performed
- Discuss any findings (deck repairs, additional work)
- Review warranty information
- Receive lien waivers and final paperwork
- Make final payment

### Duration: 1-2 hours

## Weather-Related Delays

### Conditions That Delay Work:
- **Active precipitation** (rain, snow)
- **High winds** (over 40-50 mph)
- **Extreme temperatures** (below 20°F or above 100°F)
- **Lightning** within the area

### How Delays Are Handled:
- Most contracts have weather delay clauses
- Work resumes when conditions improve
- Material storage is protected
- You're notified of revised schedule

## After Installation: First 30 Days

### Week 1:
- **Check attic** for any signs of leaks
- **Verify all debris** has been cleared from yard
- **Monitor gutters** during first rain
- **Keep warranty paperwork** in safe place

### Week 2-4:
- **Monitor for loose shingles** after high winds
- **Check for granule loss** in gutters (some is normal)
- **Document any concerns** in writing to contractor
- **Register manufacturer warranty** if required

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I stay in my home during roof replacement?
Yes, most homeowners stay during replacement. However, expect significant noise, especially during tear-off. Some homeowners choose to leave, especially on tear-off day.

### What happens if it rains during installation?
Contractors monitor weather closely. They ensure the roof is watertight before leaving each day. If unexpected rain occurs, they'll tarps your roof to protect your home.

### Will my landscaping be damaged?
Professional contractors take precautions to protect landscaping. However, plants close to the home may need to be moved or temporarily covered.

### How do they protect my property during tear-off?
They'll use tarps and plywood to protect siding, windows, and landscaping. They'll also set up trash chutes to direct debris into dumpsters or trucks.

### What if problems are found after the crew leaves?
Contact your contractor immediately. Most reputable contractors will address punch-list items promptly. Document issues with photos.
