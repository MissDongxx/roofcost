# How to Hire a Roofing Contractor: 12 Essential Tips

Your roof is a major investment, and hiring the right contractor is crucial for ensuring quality work and protecting that investment. After storm events, unscrupulous contractors often appear in affected areas. Use these tips to ensure you hire a qualified, reputable professional.

## 1. Verify Licensing and Insurance

### What to Check:

**State License**
- Verify license is current and valid
- Check for any complaints or disciplinary actions
- Confirm license covers roofing work
- Note: Some states don't require licensing, so check local requirements

**Insurance Coverage**
- **Workers' Compensation**: Protects you if worker is injured on your property
- **General Liability**: Covers damage to your property
- **Completed Operations**: Covers problems that arise after completion
- Request certificate of insurance and call to verify

### Red Flags:
❌ Contractor hesitant to provide insurance information
❌ Insurance has expired or coverage amounts seem low
❌ Not licensed in a state that requires it

## 2. Check for Manufacturer Certifications

### Why It Matters:
- Certified contractors receive advanced training
- Can offer extended warranties (some up to lifetime)
- Held to higher installation standards
- Can offer enhanced warranty options

### Major Manufacturer Programs:
- **GAF**: Master Elite® or Certified™
- **CertainTeed**: SELECT ShingleMaster™
- **Owens Corning**: Preferred Contractor
- **Tamko**: Pro Certified
- **Atlas**: Signature Select

### What to Ask:
"Are you certified by the manufacturer of the shingles you're proposing?"
"Can you offer manufacturer-backed warranties that include labor?"

## 3. Check Local Reputation and Reviews

### Where to Look:

**Online Reviews:**
- Google Reviews (look for 4.0+ stars with substantial reviews)
- Better Business Bureau (check rating and complaint history)
- Houzz, Angi, Thumbtack
- Facebook and Nextdoor

**What to Look For:**
- Overall rating (4+ stars is good)
- Number of reviews (more reviews = more reliable pattern)
- Response to negative reviews (professional resolution)
- Recency of reviews

**What to Ignore:**
- A few negative reviews among many positives
- Very old reviews (contractor may have changed)
- Reviews that seem generic or suspicious

### Offline Verification:
- Ask for references from recent projects
- Drive by completed work (with permission)
- Ask local building material suppliers for recommendations

## 4. Get Multiple Written Estimates

### How Many Estimates?
- **Minimum**: 3 estimates
- **Ideal**: 3-5 estimates
- **Too Many**: More than 6 becomes overwhelming

### Estimate Comparison Checklist:
- [ ] Material specifications (brand, type, warranty)
- [ ] Labor warranty and duration
- [ ] Square footage and squares calculated
- [ ] Tear-off included?
- [ ] Permit costs included?
- [ ] Disposal fees included?
- [ ] Payment schedule
- [ ] Timeline and start date
- [ ] Ventilation assessment
- [ ] Deck repair allowance
- [ ] Total cost breakdown

### Warning: Be Wary of:
- Estimates significantly lower than others (20%+ difference)
- Vague descriptions ("roofing materials" vs. "GAF Timberline HDZ")
- Pressure tactics ("price good only today")
- Cash-only discounts (likely unlicensed, uninsured)

## 5. Understand the Payment Schedule

### Standard Payment Terms:
- **Deposit**: 10-30% upon signing (to secure materials)
- **Material Delivery**: 20-30% when materials arrive
- **Halfway Point**: 20-30% when job is partially complete
- **Completion**: Balance upon satisfactory completion and inspection

### Red Flags:
❌ Deposit over 50% (high risk)
❌ Full payment before work begins
❌ Cash-only payments
❌ No written agreement on payment schedule

### Best Practice:
"Pay for work as it's completed, not before. Never pay in full until satisfied."

## 6. Understand the Warranty

### Two Warranties You Need:

**1. Manufacturer's Warranty** (Materials)
- Covers defects in shingles/materials
- Typically 20-50 years
- May require registration
- Usually prorated after initial period

**2. Workmanship Warranty** (Labor)
- Covers installation errors
- Typically 1-10 years (varies by contractor)
- What causes most roof failures

### What to Ask:
"What is your workmanship warranty, and what's covered?"
"If there's a problem, how do you handle warranty calls?"
"Is your warranty transferable if I sell my home?"

### Enhanced Warranties:
Some contractors can offer manufacturer-backed system warranties that cover both materials AND labor for up to 50 years. Ask about these options.

## 7. Check Physical Presence

### Why Local Matters:
- Available for warranty service
- Knowledge of local building codes
- Established relationship with suppliers
- Familiar with local weather challenges
- Part of your community

### What to Check:
- Physical address (not just PO Box)
- Local phone number
- How long in business (local history)
- Local reputation and references

### Storm Chasers Warning:
After major storms, out-of-town contractors ("storm chasers") appear. They may:
- Not be familiar with local codes
- Be gone tomorrow when problems arise
- Lack proper licensing/insurance
- Offer "too good to be true" deals

## 8. Ask About Their Crew

### Who Will Actually Install Your Roof?

**Questions to Ask:**
- Do you use employees or subcontractors?
- Who supervises the work?
- How many years of experience does the crew have?
- Is the same crew from start to finish?

### Why It Matters:
- Employees vs. subcontractors affects quality control
- Experienced crew = better installation
- Consistent crew = fewer problems
- On-site supervisor = better communication

## 9. Get Everything in Writing

### Your Contract Should Include:

**Scope of Work:**
- Exact materials (brand, type, color)
- Tear-off or layover (specify)
- Ventilation requirements
- Flashing replacement locations
- Deck repair process

**Timeline:**
- Start date
- Expected completion date
- Contingency for weather

**Costs:**
- Total cost breakdown
- Payment schedule
- Allowances for unforeseen work (with caps)
- Permit costs

**Warranties:**
- Workmanship warranty details
- Manufacturer warranty information
- What voids warranties

**Legal Protections:**
- Right to terminate (with conditions)
- Dispute resolution process
- Change order procedure

### Red Flags:
❌ Verbal agreements not written in contract
❌ Vague descriptions of work
❌ No mention of warranties
❌ No dispute resolution clause

## 10. Verify Communication Style

### During the Estimating Process, Note:
- Responsiveness to calls and emails
- Clarity of explanations
- Willingness to answer questions
- Professionalism and respect
- Transparency about process

### Why It Matters:
- You'll work with this person for days or weeks
- Problems arise; good communication prevents issues
- Your comfort level matters

### Green Flags:
✅ Responds promptly (within 24 hours)
✅ Explains technical terms clearly
✅ Provides photos of similar work
✅ Offers references without hesitation
✅ Patient with questions

## 11. Understand Permit Process

### Who Handles Permits?
- **Typically**: Contractor pulls permits
- **Your Responsibility**: Verify permits are pulled
- **Final Step**: Final inspection before final payment

### Why Permits Matter:
- Ensures work meets code
- Protects your investment
- Required for insurance claims
- Required when selling home

### What to Ask:
"Are permits included in your estimate?"
"Who will pull permits?"
"Will you schedule the final inspection?"
"What happens if inspection fails?"

## 12. Trust Your Instincts

### Green Flags You've Found the Right Contractor:
- You feel comfortable asking questions
- They listen to your concerns
- They offer options, not just one solution
- They explain both pros and cons
- They respect your budget
- Their reputation is solid locally
- They're not pressuring you

### Red Flags to Walk Away:
- You feel pressured or uncomfortable
- They dismiss your questions or concerns
- They're vague about details
- Price seems too good to be true
- No local presence or reputation
- Poor communication
- You can't verify licensing/insurance

## Questions to Ask Every Contractor

1. "Are you licensed and insured? Can I see proof?"
2. "How long have you been in business in this area?"
3. "Are you certified by the manufacturer?"
4. "What is your workmanship warranty?"
5. "Who will be on-site supervising my job?"
6. "Will you pull permits?"
7. "What is your payment schedule?"
8. "How do you handle unforeseen problems?"
9. "Can you provide references from recent projects?"
10. "Do you use employees or subcontractors?"

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I always choose the lowest bid?
No, the lowest bid often means cutting corners somewhere—materials, labor, insurance, or proper installation. Choose the best value, not just the lowest price.

### How long should a roofing estimate take?
A thorough estimate should take 30-60 minutes. Anything less may indicate they're not being thorough. They should measure the roof, assess its condition, and discuss options.

### Is it OK to hire a contractor who asks for cash payment?
Generally, no. Cash-only contractors often lack proper licensing and insurance. Without paper trail, you have little recourse if problems arise.

### What if a contractor says I don't need a permit?
Verify with your local building department. Most roof replacements require permits. A contractor who says otherwise may be cutting corners or operating illegally.

### How long should I wait for an estimate once I contact a contractor?
1-2 weeks is reasonable. Much longer may indicate they're overwhelmed or not very responsive. If they're slow to respond before they have your money, they'll likely be slower after.
