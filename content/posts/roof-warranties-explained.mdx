# Understanding Roof Warranties: What's Actually Covered

Roof warranties can be confusing, but understanding what's covered—and what isn't—can save you thousands in unexpected repair costs. Here's everything you need to know about roofing warranties.

## Types of Roof Warranties

### 1. Manufacturer's Material Warranty (Product Warranty)

**What It Covers**: Defects in the roofing materials themselves

**Provided By**: The shingle/material manufacturer (GAF, CertainTeed, Owens Corning, etc.)

**Typical Coverage**:
- **3-Tab Shingles**: 20-30 years
- **Architectural Shingles**: 30-50 years
- **Premium/Lifetime Shingles**: 50 years to lifetime
- **Metal Roofing**: 30-50 years
- **Tile/Slate**: 50 years to lifetime

**What's Typically Covered**:
- Manufacturing defects
- Premature granule loss
- Shingle splitting or cracking
- Color inconsistencies
- Wind speed up to specified limit (usually 110-130 mph)

**What's NOT Covered**:
- Installation errors
- Normal weathering and wear
- Damage from foot traffic
- Ponding water
- Improper ventilation
- Acts of nature (hail, hurricanes beyond wind rating)

### 2. Workmanship Warranty (Installation Warranty)

**What It Covers**: Errors in installation

**Provided By**: The roofing contractor

**Typical Coverage**:
- **Standard**: 1-2 years
- **Good**: 5-10 years
- **Excellent**: 10-25 years

**What's Typically Covered**:
- Leaks caused by installation errors
- Flashing failures
- Improper nail placement
- Sealing failures around penetrations
- Ventilation installation errors

**What's NOT Covered**:
- Material defects (covered by manufacturer)
- Damage from foot traffic
- Problems caused by homeowner modifications
- Failures due to inadequate ventilation

### 3. System Warranty (Enhanced Warranty)

**What It Covers**: Combined materials and labor when using a complete roofing system

**Provided By**: Manufacturer (requires certified contractor)

**Typical Coverage**: 20-50 years, some lifetime

**Requirements**:
- Use all components from the same manufacturer
- Installation by certified contractor
- Registration within specified timeframe
- Attic ventilation meeting manufacturer's requirements

**Benefits**:
- Covers both materials AND labor for entire warranty period
- Often non-prorated
- May include tear-off costs if replacement is needed

## Warranty Coverage Comparison

| Warranty Type | Who Provides | Covers Labor | Covers Materials | Typical Duration |
|---------------|--------------|--------------|------------------|------------------|
| Material Only | Manufacturer | No | Yes | 20-50 years |
| Workmanship | Contractor | Yes | No | 1-25 years |
| System Warranty | Manufacturer | Yes | Yes | 20-lifetime |

## Understanding Prorated vs. Non-Prorated Warranties

### Non-Prorated Warranty
The manufacturer pays 100% of covered defects for the entire warranty period.

**Example**: 50-year non-prorated warranty—If shingles fail in year 30, manufacturer pays full replacement cost.

### Prorated Warranty
Coverage decreases over time. You pay a portion of replacement costs based on the shingle's age.

**Example**: 50-year prorated warranty—If shingles fail in year 30:
- Years 1-10: 100% covered
- Years 11-20: 80% covered
- Years 21-30: 60% covered
- You pay 40% of replacement cost

**Most architectural shingles now offer non-prorated coverage for the full warranty period.**

## Common Warranty Exclusions

### Almost All Warranties Exclude:

1. **Improper Ventilation**
   - Inadequate intake or exhaust ventilation
   - Improper ventilation balance
   - Can void entire warranty

2. **Installation Errors**
   - Unless covered by system warranty
   - Contractor's workmanship warranty should address this

3. **Foot Traffic Damage**
   - Walking on roof for maintenance
   - HVAC service, solar installation, etc.
   - Satellite dish installation

4. **Improper Repairs or Modifications**
   - DIY repairs
   - Adding solar panels without proper mounting
   - Installing equipment without proper flashing

5. **Ponding Water**
   - Water that stands on roof for more than 48 hours
   - Usually due to design or drainage issues

6. **Acts of Nature**
   - Hail (may be covered by separate hail warranty)
   - Hurricanes beyond wind speed rating
   - Tornadoes, earthquakes

7. **Failure to Register**
   - Some warranties require registration within 60 days
   - Missing deadline can reduce coverage period

## Warranty Registration

### Why Register:
- Activates full warranty coverage
- May extend coverage period
- Creates record of purchase
- Required for some enhanced warranties

### How to Register:
- Online through manufacturer's website
- Mail-in card included with shingles
- Contractor may register on your behalf

### Timing:
- Usually within 30-60 days of installation
- Don't wait—set a reminder

## What Void Your Warranty

### Installation-Related Voids:
- Not using a certified contractor (for system warranties)
- Improper attic ventilation
- Installing new shingles over old ones (when not allowed)
- Not using required accessories

### Maintenance-Related Voids:
- Pressure washing shingles
- Using harsh chemicals
- Installing equipment without proper flashing
- Failing to address leaks promptly

### Documentation-Related Voids:
- Failure to register warranty
- No proof of purchase
- No proof of proper installation

## Making a Warranty Claim

### Step 1: Document the Issue
- Take clear photos and videos
- Note when problem started
- Document any weather events that may have contributed

### Step 2: Contact Your Contractor First
- For issues within workmanship warranty period
- Most reputable contractors will address their own work

### Step 3: File Manufacturer Claim
- If contractor is unresponsive or out of business
- For material defects outside workmanship warranty
- Include photos, proof of purchase, and installation details

### Step 4: Manufacturer Inspection
- Manufacturer sends representative
- Determines if claim is valid
- Approves or denies claim

## Transferability

### Can Warranty Be Transferred to New Owners?

**Material Warranty**: Usually yes, but:
- May require registration within first 60 days
- Some limit transfer to one time
- Some reduce coverage period for second owner

**Workmanship Warranty**: Usually no, but:
- Some premium contractors offer transferable workmanship warranty
- May require transfer fee

**System Warranty**: Often transferable:
- GAF Golden Pledge: Transferable once within 20 years
- Owens Corning: Transferable to subsequent owners

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are "lifetime" warranties really lifetime?
"Lifetime" warranties typically mean for as long as you own the home, or a fixed period like 50 years, whichever is longer. It's not truly your lifetime.

### Does roof warranty cover hail damage?
Standard warranties don't cover hail damage. You need separate hail coverage (some manufacturers offer it) or homeowners insurance. Impact-resistant shingles may offer better hail protection.

### What if my contractor goes out of business?
The material warranty remains valid regardless. For workmanship issues, you're out of luck unless you purchased a manufacturer-backed system warranty that includes labor.

### Is roof warranty worth paying extra for?
For system warranties that include labor, yes—they can save you thousands if problems arise. For standard warranties, they're included in the shingle price.

### How can I ensure my warranty remains valid?
1. Hire a reputable, certified contractor
2. Ensure proper ventilation
3. Register warranty promptly
4. Keep all documentation
5. Avoid walking on roof unnecessarily
6. Address problems promptly
