## Asphalt Shingles

### 3-Tab Shingles
**Best For**: Budget projects, rental properties

**Pros:**
- Most affordable option
- Wide variety of colors
- Easy to repair
- Lightweight (no structural reinforcement needed)

**Cons:**
- Shortest lifespan (15-20 years)
- Lower wind resistance
- Can look less premium

**Cost**: $3.50-5.50 per sq.ft

### Architectural (Laminated) Shingles
**Best For**: Most residential applications

**Pros:**
- Dimensional appearance mimics wood shake
- Better wind resistance (up to 130 mph)
- Longer warranty (30-50 years)
- Good value for money

**Cons:**
- More expensive than 3-tab
- Heavier (may require roof deck reinforcement)

**Cost**: $4.50-7.50 per sq.ft

## Metal Roofing

### Standing Seam Metal
**Best For**: Premium homes, harsh weather areas

**Pros:**
- Exceptional longevity (50+ years)
- Energy efficient (reflective coating available)
- Fire resistant
- Lightweight
- Low maintenance

**Cons:**
- Highest initial cost
- Can be noisy during rain (without proper insulation)
- Dent potential in hail

**Cost**: $10-18 per sq.ft

### Corrugated/R-Panel Metal
**Best For**: Agricultural buildings, modern homes, accent roofs

**Pros:**
- More affordable than standing seam
- Durable and weather resistant
- Easy installation
- Modern aesthetic

**Cons:**
- Exposed fasteners (potential leak points)
- Less premium appearance

**Cost**: $7-12 per sq.ft

## Tile Roofing

### Concrete Tile
**Best For**: Spanish, Mission, Mediterranean style homes

**Pros:**
- Excellent durability (50+ years)
- Fire resistant
- Great thermal mass (energy efficiency)
- Variety of styles and colors
- Low maintenance

**Cons:**
- Very heavy (requires structural reinforcement)
- Higher cost
- Can crack under foot traffic

**Cost**: $9-18 per sq.ft

### Clay Tile
**Best For**: Luxury homes in warm climates

**Pros:**
- Beautiful aesthetic
- Extremely long lifespan (75+ years)
- Fire and insect resistant
- Color doesn't fade

**Cons:**
- Most expensive tile option
- Heaviest material (structural reinforcement required)
- Fragile to hail

**Cost**: $12-20 per sq.ft

## Slate Roofing

**Best For**: Historic homes, luxury properties, lifetime investment

**Pros:**
- Unmatched longevity (100+ years)
- Beautiful natural appearance
- Fire resistant
- Increases home value significantly
- Environmentally friendly (natural stone)

**Cons:**
- Highest material and installation cost
- Requires specialized installation
- Heavy (structural reinforcement needed)
- Difficult to repair

**Cost**: $15-30 per sq.ft

## Synthetic & Composite Roofing

**Best For**: Homeowners wanting premium look with less weight

**Pros:**
- Mimics slate, shake, or tile at lower cost
- Lightweight (no reinforcement needed)
- Good durability (40-50 years)
- Impact resistant
- Low maintenance

**Cons:**
- Newer technology (long-term track record limited)
- Can be expensive compared to asphalt
- Color selection may be limited

**Cost**: $8-14 per sq.ft

## Choosing the Right Material

### By Climate
- **Hot/Sunny**: Metal or tile with reflective coatings
- **Cold/Snowy**: Metal (snow slides off easily)
- **Coastal**: Aluminum or copper metal (salt resistant)
- **Hail-prone**: Impact-resistant synthetic or metal

### By Budget
- **Under $10,000**: Asphalt architectural shingles
- **$10,000-20,000**: Premium architectural shingles or metal
- **$20,000-30,000**: Standing seam metal or tile
- **$30,000+**: Slate or premium synthetic

### By Home Style
- **Traditional**: Architectural shingles
- **Spanish/Mediterranean**: Clay or concrete tile
- **Modern**: Standing seam metal
- **Historic**: Natural slate or synthetic slate
- **Farmhouse/Rustic**: Metal corrugated or wood shake

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most popular roofing material?
Asphalt architectural shingles are the most popular choice, covering approximately 70% of American homes due to their balance of cost, durability, and appearance.

### Which roofing material lasts the longest?
Natural slate has the longest lifespan at 100+ years, followed by clay tile at 75+ years and metal roofing at 50+ years.

### Is metal roofing worth the extra cost?
Yes, if you plan to stay in your home long-term. Metal roofing can last 2-3 times longer than asphalt, making it cost-effective over time.

### What roofing material adds the most home value?
Slate and premium metal roofing typically add the most value, both in actual home value increase and buyer appeal.

### Can I install metal roofing over shingles?
In many cases, yes, depending on local building codes and roof condition. This can save on tear-off costs.
