# 10 Signs You Need a New Roof: Don't Wait Until It's Too Late

Your roof is your home's first line of defense against the elements. Knowing when it's time for replacement can save you thousands in water damage repairs. Here are the telltale signs that your roof needs attention.

## 1. Shingles Are Curling, Cupping, or Clawing

**What to look for:**
- **Curling**: Shingle edges turn upward
- **Cupping**: Shingle centers are raised with edges flat
- **Clawing**: Shingle edges curl downward

**What it means**: Your shingles have reached the end of their lifespan and are no longer providing adequate protection.

**Action**: Plan for replacement within 1-2 years.

## 2. Bald Spots and Missing Granules

**What to look for:**
- Dark patches on shingles where granules are missing
- Exposed fiberglass mat
- Excessive granules in gutters and downspouts

**What it means**: Granules protect shingles from UV rays. Once they're gone, asphalt deteriorates rapidly.

**Action**: If bald spots cover more than 20% of the roof, replacement is needed.

## 3. Cracked Shingles

**What to look for:**
- Cracks running through shingles
- Multiple cracked shingles in the same area

**What it means**: Cracks allow water penetration and indicate shingle failure from age or weather damage.

**Action**: If cracks are widespread, replace the roof.

## 4. Missing Shingles

**What to look for:**
- Gaps in your roof where shingles are completely gone
- Visible roofing nails or exposed underlayment

**What it means**: Missing shingles create direct pathways for water to enter your home.

**Action**: Immediate repair is needed. If missing shingles are widespread, consider replacement.

## 5. Moss or Algae Growth

**What to look for:**
- Green, fuzzy moss growth on shingles
- Black streaks (algae) running down the roof

**What it means**: While algae is mostly cosmetic, moss can lift shingles and trap moisture, causing premature decay.

**Action**: Clean moss immediately. If it returns quickly or has caused damage, replacement may be needed.

## 6. Sagging Roof Deck

**What to look for:**
- Visible dips or sags in the roof plane
- Warped or uneven appearance from the ground

**What it means**: This indicates structural problems—rotting deck, damaged rafters, or foundation issues.

**Action**: **URGENT**—contact a roofing professional immediately. This is a serious structural issue.

## 7. Daylight Through Roof Boards

**What to look for:**
- Light visible through roof boards when in the attic
- Sunbeams coming through visible cracks

**What it means**: Holes in your roof deck allow water, pests, and air infiltration.

**Action**: **URGENT**—immediate professional inspection required.

## 8. Water Stains on Ceilings or Walls

**What to look for:**
- Brown or yellowish stains on ceilings
- Stains appearing along walls near ceiling
- Peeling paint or wallpaper

**What it means**: Active or recurring roof leaks. Even small stains can indicate significant problems.

**Action**: Immediate professional inspection needed.

## 9. Your Roof Is Over 20 Years Old

**What to look for:**
- Original roof (never replaced)
- Records showing installation date

**What it means**: Most asphalt shingle roofs last 20-25 years. Even if visible damage isn't apparent, the roof is near failure.

**Action**: Schedule professional inspection. Plan for replacement if recommended.

## 10. Neighbors Are Replacing Their Roofs

**What to look for:**
- Multiple homes in your neighborhood getting new roofs
- Homes built around the same time as yours

**What it means**: If your neighbors' roofs are failing, yours likely has similar issues due to same materials, installation, and weather exposure.

**Action**: Schedule a professional inspection to assess your roof's condition.

## When to Repair vs. Replace

### Repair Is Appropriate When:
- Damage is limited to a small area (less than 25%)
- Roof is relatively young (less than 15 years)
- Problem is isolated (e.g., wind damage, tree branch)
- Budget is tight and you can buy 2-3 more years

### Replacement Is Necessary When:
- Damage is widespread (more than 30-35%)
- Roof is nearing end of lifespan (20+ years for asphalt)
- Multiple layers of roofing already exist
- Repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement cost
- You plan to sell in the next 5 years

## Professional Inspection Checklist

When hiring a roofing inspector, they should:

- [ ] Check all flashing (chimneys, vents, valleys)
- [ ] Inspect attic for moisture, mold, or light penetration
- [ ] Examine shingle condition across entire roof
- [ ] Check gutters and downspouts
- [ ] Evaluate ventilation system
- [ ] Assess roof deck condition
- [ ] Look for signs of animal damage
- [ ] Document findings with photos
- [ ] Provide written report with recommendations

## Emergency Warning Signs

**Call a professional immediately if you notice:**

1. Water actively dripping into your home
2. Sagging roof sections
3. Large sections of missing shingles after a storm
4. Debris on the ground after heavy weather
5. Sudden increase in energy bills (indicates ventilation failure)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I inspect my own roof?
You can do a visual inspection from the ground using binoculars, but walking on your roof is dangerous and can cause damage. Always hire a professional for close-up inspection.

### How often should I have my roof inspected?
Have a professional inspection every 3-5 years, and after any major storm event with high winds or hail.

### Will insurance pay for a new roof?
It depends on the cause. Sudden damage from storms is usually covered, while wear and tear from age is typically not covered.

### How long can I wait once I notice problems?
Don't wait. Small problems become large ones quickly. Even a minor leak can cause mold, rot, and structural damage within months.

### Should I get multiple opinions?
Yes, especially for expensive replacements. Get 2-3 quotes from licensed, insured contractors before making a decision.
